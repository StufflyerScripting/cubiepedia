Getting Started

Solving a 4x4x4 puzzle blindfolded is probably easier than you think, if you already know how to solve a 3x3x3 blindfolded. You’ll need to memorize around 25 letter pairs, which you can efficiently do using Roman Rooms. There aren't much algorithms to learn, enjoy and good luck!


Requirements:

  • M2 for edges
  • OP for corners
  • Time and dedication

    • Swapping Algorithms

  • Center: U2
  • Wing: r2

    • Swapping Rules

  • For most cases, use: setup + swap + undo setup
  • For special cases, use only the algorithm shown
  • Centers: If B or D is second in a letter pair, swap it to the other
  • Wings: If I or S is second in a letter pair, swap it to the other

    • Parity Algorithms

  • Center: U2
  • Wing: r' U2 r U2 r' U2 x r U2 r U2 r U2 r2 U2 x' r' U2
  • Corner: U2 R U R' U' (r2 U2 r2 Uw2 r2 Uw2) U' R U' R' U2

    • Parity Rules

  • If the number of targets (letters) is odd for a step, do the parity algorithm after solving it
  • Parity is independent per step (centers, wings, corners)
  • Execution order: Centers → Wings → Corners (important to avoid breaking pieces)

    • Center Setup Moves

  • A: buffer
  • B: (special) Rw' F' U r U' l' U r' U' l F Rw U2
  • C: none
  • D: (special) Lw F' r U l' U' r' U l U' F Lw' U2
  • E: r u r'
  • F: y' u r' u' r
  • G: r' d r
  • H: y' r d2 r'
  • I: r u2 r'
  • J: y' r' u r
  • K: d' r' d r
  • L: y' r d r'
  • M: r u' r'
  • N: y' r' u2 r
  • O: r' d' r
  • P: y' d' r d r'
  • Q: u r u' r'
  • R: y' r' u' r
  • S: r' d2 r
  • T: y' r d' r'
  • U: (special) D [swap to V] D'
  • V: (special) Lw' U' r2 U' l U r2 U' l' U2 Lw U2
  • W: (special) D' [swap to V] D
  • X: (special) D2 [swap to V] D2

    • Wing Setup Moves

  • A: none
  • B: R U R' U'

  • E: B L' B'

  • H: L B L' B'
  • I: (special) D r U R2 U' r' U R2 U' D' r2
  • J: U R U'
  • K: (special) l2 [swap to Q] l2
  • L: U' L' U
  • M: B' R B
  • N: R' B' R B
  • O: B' R' B
  • P: B' R2 B

  • S: (special) r2 D U R2 U' r U R2 U' r' D'
  • T: U R' U'

  • V: U R2 U'
